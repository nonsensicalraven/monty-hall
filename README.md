<h1> Monty Hall Problem </h1>

You are invited to a game show. The premise is to win a car:
- there are three doors
- two of them contain goats and the other contains a car
- You choose one door initially
- the host, Monty Hall opens a door containing a goat, out of the other two doors
- You are given a choice. you can either switch your door with the other or stay with your original door

 What do you do? 


We might think that switching is irrelevant and the chances of winning the car no matter if you switch or not are 33% but this is not the case

There are greater chances at winning if you choose to switch. a simple explanation you can follow intuitively is:  
Think of the doors.  
Yes, the chances of picking the door with the car is 33% and chances of picking a door with a goat is 66%.  
there is higher chnace of you choosing wrong initially.  
Regardless, all we want is to end up choosing the door with the car. 

<h3>Think of it this way: </h3>

Switching always gets the result of what you originally chose.  
Choose bad door originally = end up with good door  
Choose good door origibally = end up with bad door  

Since it is more likely that you chose the wrong door initially, switching is more likely to get you to the right door
